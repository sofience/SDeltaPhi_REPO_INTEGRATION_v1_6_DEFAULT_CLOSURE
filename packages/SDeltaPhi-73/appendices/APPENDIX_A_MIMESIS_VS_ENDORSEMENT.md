# Appendix A — Mimesis vs Endorsement

A creative work often represents actions, beliefs, or desires that the work does not endorse.

## Core distinctions

```text
depiction ≠ endorsement
representation ≠ recommendation
character viewpoint ≠ authorial claim
narrative presence ≠ moral approval
reader discomfort ≠ direct harm
```

## Why it matters

If mimesis and endorsement collapse, art becomes morally flat. Villains cannot be convincing, tragedy cannot be painful, satire cannot be dangerous, horror cannot be horrifying, and social critique cannot show the thing it critiques.

A society that cannot tolerate representation cannot understand what it refuses to represent.
