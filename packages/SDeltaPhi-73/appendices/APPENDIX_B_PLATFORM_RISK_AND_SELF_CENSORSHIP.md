# Appendix B — Platform Risk and Creator Self-Censorship

Flattening often occurs without explicit censorship.

## Path

```text
controversial expression
→ possible complaint
→ possible platform risk
→ possible advertiser/legal cost
→ creator anticipates cost
→ creator self-censors
→ content becomes safer before publication
```

## Key point

The strongest censorship may happen before any moderator acts. It happens when creators internalize the platform's expected risk function.

## SΔϕ interpretation

Self-censorship is a path-cost adaptation. The creator avoids high-cost expressive routes and selects safer defaults. Over time, this changes the topology of culture.
