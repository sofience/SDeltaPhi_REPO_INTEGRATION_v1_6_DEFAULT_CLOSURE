# Appendix D — Reader Interpretive Muscle

Creative freedom depends not only on creators and platforms, but also on readers/audiences.

## Interpretive muscle includes

- distinguishing depiction from endorsement
- separating character viewpoint from authorial claim
- recognizing irony, satire, unreliable narration, and genre convention
- tolerating discomfort long enough to interpret
- asking where cost is shown or hidden
- distinguishing moral disturbance from direct harm

## Loss of interpretive muscle

When audiences repeatedly consume flattened content, difficult works become harder to read. Ambiguity is treated as error, discomfort as harm, villainy as endorsement, and unresolved conflict as bad writing.

## Cultural consequence

Flattened readers demand flattened works. Flattened works produce flattened readers.
