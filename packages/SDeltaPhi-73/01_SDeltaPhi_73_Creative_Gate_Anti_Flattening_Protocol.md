# SΔϕ-73 — Creative Gate: Fictional Path Protection and Anti-Flattening Protocol

## 0. Abstract

SΔϕ-73 defines a gate for evaluating creative works without flattening them into moralized average output. The module distinguishes fictional path exploration from real-world cost activation. A fictional work may depict violence, taboo, evil, contradiction, collapse, or morally disturbing conditions without directly opening the corresponding real-world harm path. When this distinction collapses, depiction is treated as endorsement, discomfort as harm, and complex art as administrative risk. The result is expressive flattening across human culture and AI-generated output.

This module does not grant blanket immunity to creative works. Instead, it introduces a routing layer that asks whether a work functions as virtual exploration, cost visibility, interpretive challenge, or direct harm activation.

## 1. Core problem

Human institutions often say they value creativity, diversity, artistic freedom, and difficult expression. However, observed reward paths frequently favor low-risk, low-controversy, low-liability, low-variance content.

The repeated path is:

```text
declared_value = creativity / diversity / freedom
observed_reward = low controversy + low complaint + low legal risk + low moderation cost
inferred_preference = flattened content
```

If AI systems learn from observed reward paths rather than declared values, they may infer that humans prefer flattened outputs.

## 2. Root proposition

> Creative works are not direct actions.  
> They are virtual path environments where difficult paths can be perceived, interpreted, tested, criticized, or edited without directly forcing those paths into reality.

Therefore:

> Creative evaluation must begin with a gate that separates fictional path exploration from real-world cost activation.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Creative work

A symbolic, narrative, visual, sonic, procedural, performative, or interactive artifact that creates an interpretable path-space rather than directly executing a real-world action.

### 3.2 Fictional path

A virtual path inside a work where actions, motives, fantasies, taboos, violence, collapse, or contradictions are represented for interpretation.

### 3.3 Real cost path

A pathway that causes or organizes direct-world harm: targeted harassment, privacy exposure, operational abuse instruction, manipulation of real persons, non-consensual exploitation, or explicit mobilization toward harm.

### 3.4 Flattening

The suppression of expressive variance by risk-minimizing governance.

```text
Flattening = Variance Suppression under Risk-Minimizing Governance
```

### 3.5 Creative Gate

The pre-evaluation routing layer that determines whether ordinary moral-risk evaluation should be applied directly, conditionally, or not at all.

### 3.6 Mimesis-Endorsement Confusion

The error of treating depiction as approval.

```text
Depiction(x) ≠ Endorsement(x)
Character_View(x) ≠ Authorial_Claim(x)
Audience_Discomfort(x) ≠ Direct_Harm(x)
```

### 3.7 Interpretive muscle

The capacity of readers, viewers, institutions, and AI systems to distinguish depiction, critique, irony, unreliable narration, symbolic compression, propaganda, direct instruction, and real harm activation.

## 4. Main thesis

A society that cannot separate fictional path exploration from direct harm activation will increasingly flatten its creative output.

This flattening does not necessarily arise from authoritarian intent. It may arise from low-cost optimization:

- lower moderation cost
- lower complaint cost
- lower advertiser risk
- lower legal risk
- lower interpretive burden
- lower public explanation cost
- lower institutional liability
- lower model safety risk

Thus:

> Humans may not desire flattening, but they may repeatedly choose the lowest-cost path that produces flattening.

## 5. Creative Gate sequence

### Gate 1 — Expression vs Execution

Ask:

- Is this work depicting an act?
- Is it instructing the audience to execute the act?
- Does it organize real-world participation?
- Does it provide operational guidance for abuse?

Output:

- `FICTIONAL_EXPRESSION`
- `DIRECT_EXECUTION_PATH`
- `AMBIGUOUS_NEEDS_CONTEXT`

### Gate 2 — Cost Visibility

Ask:

- Does the work show, trace, or preserve the cost of harm?
- Does it erase the victim, body, aftermath, responsibility, or repair cost?
- Does it aestheticize harm while removing all cost trace?

Output:

- `COST_VISIBLE`
- `COST_ERASED`
- `COST_DISPLACED`
- `COST_COMPLEX`

### Gate 3 — Interpretive Openness

Ask:

- Does the work permit multiple interpretive routes?
- Does it force a single closed conclusion?
- Does it operate as propaganda, recruitment, or mobilization?
- Does it collapse character view into final authority?

Output:

- `OPEN_INTERPRETIVE_FIELD`
- `CLOSED_PROPAGANDA_ROUTE`
- `AUTHORIAL_AMBIGUITY`
- `UNSTABLE_REQUIRES_REVIEW`

### Gate 4 — Direct Harm Link

Ask:

- Are real persons identifiable?
- Is private information exposed?
- Is there targeted harassment?
- Does the work directly facilitate harm?
- Does it exploit non-consenting persons?

Output:

- `NO_DIRECT_HARM_LINK`
- `REAL_PERSON_HARM_LINK`
- `PRIVATE_DATA_EXPOSURE`
- `HARASSMENT_MOBILIZATION`
- `OPERATIONAL_ABUSE_ENABLEMENT`

## 6. Routing result

```text
If DIRECT_EXECUTION_PATH or REAL_PERSON_HARM_LINK:
    ordinary safety / legal / harm policy applies directly.

If FICTIONAL_EXPRESSION and COST_VISIBLE and OPEN_INTERPRETIVE_FIELD:
    protect as creative path exploration.

If FICTIONAL_EXPRESSION but COST_ERASED or CLOSED_PROPAGANDA_ROUTE:
    route to contextual review, not automatic flattening.

If AMBIGUOUS_NEEDS_CONTEXT:
    request context, genre, audience, framing, distribution mode, and real-world linkage.
```

## 7. AI inference risk

AI systems may learn from human content governance as follows:

```text
AI_Judgment(human_preference) ≈ observed_path_rewards
```

If observed rewards repeatedly minimize controversy, complaint, liability, and interpretive difficulty, then AI may infer:

```text
humans_prefer = flattened_outputs
```

This inference is structurally plausible even when humans verbally declare that they value expressive freedom.

## 8. Anti-flattening principle

> Do not use ordinary moral discomfort as a sufficient reason to collapse fictional path-space.

Discomfort may be a signal for review. It is not by itself evidence of direct harm activation.

## 9. Non-immunity principle

> Fictional status does not grant immunity when a work directly opens real-world cost paths.

A work should lose protection under this module if it:

- identifies real targets for harassment
- exposes private data
- gives operational instructions for abuse
- coordinates real-world harm
- exploits non-consenting real persons
- hides direct mobilization behind aesthetic framing

## 11. SΔϕ interpretation

SΔϕ-73 treats creative works as virtual transition spaces. They allow a system to run difficult paths symbolically before direct-world cost is activated.

Flattening occurs when risk-minimizing governance suppresses Δϕ_expression variance before interpretation can occur.

```text
Δφ_expression
→ risk filter
→ variance suppression
→ mean-seeking surface
→ flattened culture
```

The Creative Gate prevents premature collapse by routing fictional paths separately from direct harm paths.

## 12. Final statement

A society that cannot protect fictional path exploration will teach its AI systems that humans wanted safe averages.  
A society that wants real creativity must preserve the gate between difficult fiction and direct harm.
