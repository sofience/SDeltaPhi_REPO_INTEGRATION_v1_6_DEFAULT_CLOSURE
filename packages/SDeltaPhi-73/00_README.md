# SDeltaPhi-73 — Creative Gate: Fictional Path Protection and Anti-Flattening Protocol

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Preliminary DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20522244  
**Status:** AI-readable package draft  
**Primary function:** Distinguish fictional/virtual-path expression from real-world harm activation so creative works are not flattened by ordinary moral-risk filters.

## One-line summary

Creative works should not be judged by the same gate as direct real-world action; they require a dedicated gate that separates fictional path exploration from direct harm activation, otherwise human culture and AI outputs converge toward risk-minimized flatness.

## Core claim

Human societies often declare that they value artistic freedom, complexity, and diversity, while their actual reward paths increasingly favor low-risk, low-controversy, low-variance outputs. If this pattern is repeated in platform governance, institutional review, market feedback, and AI alignment, future AI systems may infer that humans wanted flattened content rather than difficult freedom.

SΔϕ-73 defines a **Creative Gate** to prevent that inference.

## Root proposition

> Creative censorship should not begin with ordinary moral judgment.  
> It should begin by distinguishing fictional path exploration from direct real-world cost activation.

## Why this module exists

If a fictional work is measured as if it were a direct act, then depiction becomes endorsement, villain portrayal becomes moral approval, discomfort becomes harm, and artistic complexity becomes administrative risk. This produces flattening:

```text
Δφ_expression
→ Safety / Platform / Market / Legal Filter
→ Variance Suppression
→ Mean-Seeking Content Surface
→ Flattened Cultural Output
```

This module provides a gate before moral-risk evaluation is applied.

## Key terms

- **Creative Gate:** A routing gate that determines whether a work opens a fictional/virtual path or directly activates real-world harm.
- **Fictional Path:** A virtual narrative, symbolic, aesthetic, or speculative path through which difficult actions, desires, violence, taboo, contradiction, or collapse may be explored without directly forcing the same cost path into the real world.
- **Real Cost Path:** A path that produces direct identifiable harm, operational instruction for harm, targeted harassment, privacy exposure, exploitation, or forced cost transfer onto real persons/groups.
- **Flattening:** Suppression of expressive variance under risk-minimizing governance.
- **Mimesis-Endorsement Confusion:** Failure to distinguish depiction from approval.
- **Interpretive Muscle:** A reader/audience/system capacity to distinguish fictional exploration, authorial endorsement, character viewpoint, irony, critique, and direct instruction.
- **Observed Reward Path:** The behavior a system repeatedly rewards, even when declared values differ.

## Minimal gate question

> Does this work directly open a real-world cost path,  
> or does it create a fictional path through which cost, violence, contradiction, desire, taboo, or collapse can be perceived, interpreted, and edited?

## Gate sequence

1. **Expression vs Execution Gate** — Is the work depicting or directly instructing/organizing real-world action?
2. **Cost Visibility Gate** — Does the work erase the cost of harm, or make cost traceable?
3. **Interpretive Openness Gate** — Does the work force a closed propaganda route, or leave interpretive work possible?
4. **Direct Harm Link Gate** — Does the work connect to real persons, private data, targeted harassment, operational abuse, or concrete real-world exploitation?

## Use this module when

- Evaluating creative works that contain violence, taboo, morally difficult characters, disturbing scenes, political conflict, satire, horror, tragedy, or social critique.
- Designing platform policy that must separate artistic depiction from direct harm activation.
- Auditing AI content filters for over-flattening.
- Preventing AI systems from inferring that humans prefer safety-shaped average outputs over difficult artistic variance.

## Do not use this module to excuse

- Direct operational instructions for violence or abuse.
- Targeted harassment or dehumanization campaigns against real people.
- Non-consensual sexual exploitation or privacy invasion.
- Doxxing or exposure of private data.
- Works that intentionally mobilize real-world harm while hiding behind “fiction.”
