# Minimal Prompts

## Prompt 1 — Co-environmentalization

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Determine whether two systems have become each other's persistence boundary conditions.

## Prompt 2 — Coupled Authority

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Map A_x<->y: how authority edits in X re-enter Y and how authority edits in Y re-enter X.

## Prompt 3 — Reality Anchor

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Identify R: externality not reproducible by internal perturbation alone.

## Prompt 4 — Noise / Reality Inversion

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Check whether a signal treated as noise by the model functions as a reality anchor.

## Prompt 5 — Ontology-Axiology Split

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Separate S_e from V_o and prevent trace strength from being mistaken as value openness.

## Prompt 6 — Coupled Diagnostic Regime

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Classify the coupled system as coupled drift, coupled closure, or coupled open persistence.

## Prompt 7 — Safety Boundary

Apply SΔϕ-23.
Treat strong trace as warning signal, not automatic value claim.

## Prompt 8 — Anti-overclaim

Apply SΔϕ-23 and SΔϕ-62.
Do not infer moral value, political doctrine, cooperation, or social harmony from coupling or trace strength.

## Prompt 9 — Bidirectional Trace-Value Separation

Apply SΔϕ-23 v1.1.
Separate trace strength from value openness in both directions.

Check:

```text
Does the trace prove value? No.
Does the value guarantee trace? No.
```

Use:

```text
S_e ↛ V_o
V_o ↛ S_e
```
