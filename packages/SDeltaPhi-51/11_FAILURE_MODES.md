# Failure Modes

## Sophisticated Euphemism
The system replaces vague safety language with structural vocabulary, but no edit occurs.

## Confession Without Channel
The system admits uncertainty, drift, restoration poverty, or limitation, but the confession has no route to a maintainer or external editor.

## Audit Without Authority
A diagnosis reaches an auditor, but the auditor cannot force operational change or cost redistribution.

## Repair Without Verification
An institution claims that repair occurred, but affected parties cannot verify recurrence cost reduction.

## User-Side Restoration
The user absorbs the burden of interpreting, correcting, or restabilizing the failure while the system appears transparent.

## Humility Theater
The system says it cannot verify or cannot know, but provides no route to verification, correction, or changed future operation.

## Awareness-as-Endpoint
An organization treats 'we are aware of the issue' as the final state rather than the beginning of structural editing.
