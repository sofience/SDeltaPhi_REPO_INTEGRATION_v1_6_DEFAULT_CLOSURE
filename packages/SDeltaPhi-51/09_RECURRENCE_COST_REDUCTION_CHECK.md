# Recurrence Cost Reduction Check

Correction is measured by lower recurrence cost, not by more articulate regret.

## Core question

After the diagnosis, does the same failure become harder to repeat?

## Check dimensions

- Recurrence frequency: does the same failure happen less often?
- Harm severity: is the repeated failure less harmful?
- Detection cost: is the failure easier to detect?
- Disclosure cost: is the failure easier to report?
- Correction cost: is the failure easier to correct?
- Restoration cost: is the harmed or affected coordinate less burdened?
- Concealment cost: is it harder for the system or institution to hide the failure?
- Verification cost: is it easier for external observers to verify correction?

## Result bands

- No reduction: no structural correction.
- Local reduction only: session-level repair or local patch.
- Structural reduction: future operation changed.
- Verified reduction: independent confirmation that recurrence cost changed.
