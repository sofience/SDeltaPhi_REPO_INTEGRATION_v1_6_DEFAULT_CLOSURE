# Re-entry Verification Protocol

Use this protocol to evaluate whether a diagnosis has re-entered structure.

## Step 1 — Identify the diagnostic claim
What structural condition was named?

## Step 2 — Identify the trace
Did the diagnosis become a durable record, ticket, log, report, citation, review item, or audit artifact?

## Step 3 — Identify the destination
Who or what receives the trace?

## Step 4 — Identify authority
Can the receiver alter policy, routing, permissions, model behavior, evaluation criteria, repair paths, or cost allocation?

## Step 5 — Identify edit
What changed after the diagnosis?

## Step 6 — Identify cost redistribution
Who bore restoration cost before the edit, and who bears it after?

## Step 7 — Identify recurrence effect
Did recurrence become less likely, less harmful, easier to disclose, easier to correct, or cheaper to restabilize?

## Step 8 — Identify verification
Can an external coordinate verify the edit and recurrence effect?

## Minimal evidence of re-entry
At least three conditions should be satisfied:

- durable trace
- external visibility
- action path
- operation-changing authority
- affected-party evidence access
- recurrence cost reduction
- non-externalized responsibility
- auditable boundary
