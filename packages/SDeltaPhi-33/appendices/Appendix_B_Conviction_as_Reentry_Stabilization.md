# Appendix B — Conviction as Re-entry Stabilization

Conviction appears when repeated re-entry thickens belief into a directional axis. It is not dogmatism by default; it becomes risky when exception signals are systematically under-weighted.

## Illustrative uses

- Repeated experimental success stabilizes a research direction.
- Repeated political or ethical experience stabilizes long-range orientation.
- Repeated failure or success causes an organization to prefer one path over alternatives.

## Diagnostic caution

Conviction should be audited for exception handling. Directionality is adaptive when exceptions remain live and dangerous when exception re-entry is suppressed.
