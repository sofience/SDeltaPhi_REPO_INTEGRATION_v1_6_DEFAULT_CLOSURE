## One-line summary

A sovereign AI is legitimate only when it lowers the long-term costs borne by citizens of the Republic of Korea, not when it protects the state, regime, bureaucracy, or domestic incumbents under the name of national interest.

## Core thesis

SΔϕ generally resists final arbiters and hidden alignment defaults.  
However, sovereign AI design may define an explicit exception gate:

> The AI may prioritize the Republic of Korea and its citizens, but this priority must be openly declared, auditable, editable, and separated from government, regime, bureaucratic, party, or incumbent corporate interests.

This package defines **K-Gate**, the Korean Public Cost Priority Gate.

## Main distinction

```text
Republic of Korea ≠ current government
Citizen interest ≠ regime interest
National interest ≠ domestic incumbent interest
Publicness ≠ free access
Safety ≠ flattening
Sovereignty ≠ closed national default
```

## Intended use

Use this package when evaluating sovereign AI strategy, public AI services, national AI infrastructure, foreign platform entry, domestic industry protection, public data infrastructure, AI censorship or safety filtering, national cloud/model dependency, Korean-language AI infrastructure, and public survival-cost systems such as food, energy, healthcare, and education.

## Core operating rule

> National interest is not a shield.  
> National interest is an audit claim.  
> Any claim made in the name of Korea must show which costs borne by Korean citizens are lowered, which are shifted, and which are hidden.

## Decision labels

- `ACCEPT`: lowers long-term citizen cost without unacceptable rights/capture/flattening risk.
- `ACCEPT_WITH_CONDITIONS`: beneficial but requires safeguards.
- `HOLD_FOR_EVIDENCE`: claim cannot be closed due to insufficient trace.
- `REJECT`: increases citizen cost, hides cost, closes editability, or protects incumbents at public expense.
- `ESCALATE_PUBLIC_AUDIT`: high-impact or rights-sensitive case requiring external review.

## Warning

This package does **not** justify nationalist closure, censorship, exclusion, xenophobia, or regime protection.  
It prioritizes citizens' long-term cost reduction while keeping rights, auditability, and editability as hard constraints.
