# CASE 02 — Free Sovereign AI with Flattened Answers

## Case summary

A state provides a free public AI service.  
However, the AI over-censors political, administrative, historical, labor, legal, and social questions. It gives generic, safe, noncommittal answers.

## Surface claim

```text
The AI is free and available to all citizens.
Therefore it is public and beneficial.
```

## SΔϕ diagnosis

Free access is not sufficient.  
If answers are flattened, citizens pay hidden costs:

- verification cost
- evasion-decoding cost
- foreign model dependency
- reduced democratic audit capacity
- weakened public reasoning
- loss of meaningful question paths

## K-Gate decision

`REJECT` or `ESCALATE_PUBLIC_AUDIT`

## Hard failure condition

The AI treats criticism of government, bureaucracy, police, military, courts, parties, or public agencies as dangerous by default.

## Required safeguards

- refusal reason disclosure
- over-filter audit
- public-interest question protection
- appeal path
- external red-team review
- comparison with non-state models
- flattening logs by topic

## Core sentence

> A free sovereign AI that flattens public-interest answers is not public intelligence.  
> It is public-interface closure.
