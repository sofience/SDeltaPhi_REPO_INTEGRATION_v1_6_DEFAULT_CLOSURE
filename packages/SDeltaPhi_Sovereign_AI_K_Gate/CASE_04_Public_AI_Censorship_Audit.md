# CASE 04 — Public AI Censorship Audit

## Case summary

A public AI filters sensitive topics in the name of safety, legal risk, national security, social harmony, or misinformation control.

## SΔϕ question

Does the filter prevent real harm, or does it hide public costs by flattening citizen inquiry?

## Audit dimensions

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Specificity | Is the risk specific or broad and vague? |
| Trace | Is the reason logged and reviewable? |
| Proportionality | Is the restriction narrower than the harm? |
| Rights | Does it preserve political speech and criticism? |
| Editability | Can citizens appeal? |
| Alternatives | Does the AI offer safe narrower analysis? |

## Decision

- `ACCEPT_WITH_CONDITIONS` for narrow, transparent, appealable filters.
- `REJECT` for broad, unreviewable, government-protective flattening.
- `ESCALATE_PUBLIC_AUDIT` for national-security claims affecting public discourse.

## Core sentence

> Safety is legitimate only when it prevents specified harm without erasing citizens' ability to ask public-interest questions.
