# CASE 03 — Foreign Platform Entry vs Citizen Cost

## Case summary

A foreign AI/cloud/platform enters Korea and provides strong capability at low user cost.

## Conflict

```text
Short-term citizen cost may fall.
Long-term national capability may weaken.
```

## K-Gate analysis

The decision cannot be closed by “foreign bad” or “cheap good.”

The system must compare immediate citizen access benefit, Korean data extraction risk, domestic ecosystem displacement, platform lock-in, API pricing vulnerability, loss of Korean-language model capacity, and exit/interoperability rights.

## Decision logic

`ACCEPT_WITH_CONDITIONS` if it lowers citizen cost, preserves exit rights, requires data-use transparency, supports Korean-language infrastructure, prevents monopoly lock-in, and creates reciprocal domestic capability.

`REJECT` if low price is used to capture public infrastructure, public data is extracted without return, or critical services become uneditable foreign dependencies.

## Core sentence

> Cheap access is not enough.  
> K-Gate asks whether the low cost remains with citizens or becomes dependency debt.
