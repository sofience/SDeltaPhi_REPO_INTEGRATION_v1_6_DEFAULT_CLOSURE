# Abstract

**Title:** SΔϕ Sovereign AI K-Gate: Korean Public Cost Priority Framework  
**Provisional DOI:** 10.5281/zenodo.20692814  
**Version:** 1.0.0

This package defines an SΔϕ-based exception gate for sovereign AI design. The framework, called **K-Gate** or the **Korean Public Cost Priority Gate**, allows a sovereign AI to explicitly prioritize the Republic of Korea and its citizens while preventing the common collapse of “national interest” into government, regime, bureaucracy, party, security agency, or domestic incumbent interests.

The package treats sovereign AI not as a neutral chatbot, but as a public cost-routing system. It asks whether a model, platform, law, infrastructure, or policy lowers long-term costs borne by Korean citizens: food, energy, healthcare, education, data, language, security, democratic agency, and access to knowledge. It also audits whether “free public AI” merely flattens politically or socially important answers, whether domestic industry protection raises citizen costs, and whether foreign infrastructure can sometimes break incumbent rent extraction and lower public costs.

The package contains AI-native routing rules, a cost attribution matrix, evaluation rubrics, failure modes, governance/editability requirements, and case modules.
