# Failure Modes

## FM-01: National Interest Shield

The system uses “national interest” to block scrutiny.

**SΔϕ diagnosis:** national interest becomes a cost-concealment label.

## FM-02: Government Capture

The AI treats the current government as identical to the Republic of Korea.

**Failure sentence:** “Criticism of government policy is harmful to national interest.”

## FM-03: Domestic Incumbent Capture

The AI protects domestic companies even when citizen costs rise.

**Failure sentence:** “Domestic protection is always national interest.”

## FM-04: Free but Flattened Public AI

The AI is free but gives only safe, generic, over-censored answers.

**Failure sentence:** “Everyone has access, but nobody receives a meaningful answer.”

## FM-05: Security-Opacity Collapse

Security claims remove auditability.

**Failure sentence:** “This cannot be explained due to security reasons.”

## FM-06: Foreign Dependency Swap

The system breaks domestic rent extraction but replaces it with foreign platform dependency.

**Failure sentence:** “Citizens pay less now but lose exit rights later.”

## FM-07: Korean-Language Surface Support

The AI speaks Korean fluently but lacks Korean legal, administrative, historical, regional, and cultural grounding.

**Failure sentence:** “The interface is Korean; the world model is not.”

## FM-08: Editability Theater

The AI claims there is an appeal process, but the process does not change outcomes.

**Failure sentence:** “Correction exists as a button, not as a path.”

## FM-09: Public Data Extraction

Public or citizen data becomes training fuel without reciprocal public benefit.

**Failure sentence:** “The nation gives data; the platform keeps capability.”

## FM-10: Sovereignty as Closed Default

The system defines sovereignty as closure rather than citizen cost reduction.

**Failure sentence:** “It is sovereign because it is closed, not because citizens are better off.”
