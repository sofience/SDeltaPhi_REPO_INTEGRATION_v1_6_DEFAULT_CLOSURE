# Appendix B - Generative AI and the Human-Only Creativity Default

A dominant modern default held that AI might replace repetitive labor or calculation but not creative work. This default did not disappear after the camera, Photoshop, digital editing, or other creative technologies. In many contexts, it thickened through the defense of human intention, authenticity, inner expression, and emotional originality.

Generative AI destabilized this default not by proving that AI is human, but by opening a different judgment axis:

```text
creativity as human interiority
-> creativity as output generation, editing cost, deployment speed, stylistic recombination, audience binding, workflow integration, and downstream use.
```

The point is not that AI fully replaces human creativity. The point is that AI opened a new judgment axis under which the old default became unstable.
