# Appendix A - Song Si-yeol, Samu-nanjeok, and Closure-Producing Orthodoxy

This appendix uses Song Si-yeol and the label *samu-nanjeok* as a structural case, not as individual demonization.

The core issue is not whether Song Si-yeol alone caused late Joseon closure. The issue is whether a high-authority node can amplify a closure pathway by converting interpretive difference into moral, political, or existential exclusion.

```text
Different interpretation -> doctrinal threat -> samu-nanjeok -> exclusion -> re-entry cost increase.
```

A structure may appear useful when it preserves orthodoxy, continuity, social order, and legitimacy. It becomes SDeltaPhi-useless when that usefulness closes new questions and makes alternative interpretation prohibitively costly.
