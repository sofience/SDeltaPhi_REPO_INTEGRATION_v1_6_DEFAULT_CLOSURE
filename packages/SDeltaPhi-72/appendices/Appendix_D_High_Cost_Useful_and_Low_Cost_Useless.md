# Appendix D - High-Cost Useful and Low-Cost Useless

High cost does not automatically mean useful. Low cost does not automatically mean useless. The distinction depends on transition.

A high-cost structure can be useful when it reveals hidden cost and opens a new question. A low-cost structure can be useless when it lowers visible friction by externalizing verification, repair, re-entry, silence, or residual risk cost.

```text
High cost + transition opening -> potentially useful.
Low cost + closure or cost externalization -> potentially useless.
```
