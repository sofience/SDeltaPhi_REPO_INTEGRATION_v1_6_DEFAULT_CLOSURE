# Boundary Constraints and Misreadings

Do not treat diagnosis as reopening.

Do not treat critique as intervention.

Do not treat anomaly existence as trigger recognition.

Do not treat visibility as reopening.

Do not treat petition as automatic reopening.

Do not treat symbolic disruption as operational reopening.

Do not treat one-time exception as persistence of re-entry.

Do not ignore the cost asymmetry of reopening.

Do not treat reopening as spontaneous drift.

Do not claim reopening has occurred unless an alternative path is operational again.

Do not treat a reopened path as governance unless re-entry persists.

Do not use the public petition case pattern to decide the policy merits of the petition.
