# Minimal Definitions

## Closed Default

A dominant path whose revision, rollback, exception re-entry, or authority distribution has ceased to operate materially.

## Trigger Recognition

The conversion of anomaly from tolerable noise into a signal that interrupts default continuation.

## Cost Assumption

The acceptance of additional burden required to interrupt the cheapest path.

## Reopening Act

An intervention through which an excluded or suppressed alternative becomes operational again.

## Persistence of Re-entry

The durable survival of a channel through which future exceptions can continue to enter and affect the dominant structure.

## Symbolic Rupture

A visible disturbance, critique, petition, or one-time exception that does not restore an operational alternative or durable re-entry path.
