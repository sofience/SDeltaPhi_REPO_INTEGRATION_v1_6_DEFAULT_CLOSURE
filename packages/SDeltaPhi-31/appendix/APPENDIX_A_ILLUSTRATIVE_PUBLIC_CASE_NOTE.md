# Appendix A — Illustrative Public Case Note

## South Korean Gender Conscription Petition as Public Petition without Reopening

This note is an illustrative public case only.

It does not evaluate whether the proposed policy should or should not have been adopted.

It does not endorse or reject female conscription.

It uses the case only to illustrate the SΔϕ-31 distinction:

```text
petitioned exception entered visibility
≠
operational reopening occurred
```

## Case framing

During the Moon Jae-in administration in South Korea, public petitions and debate raised the question of whether the male-centered conscription default should be revisited in light of demographic decline, military manpower concerns, and perceived asymmetric burden.

Public reports describe a 2017 petition concerning joint national defense obligations and later a 2021 petition requesting that women be included in conscription review.

## SΔϕ-31 reading

```text
T_r — Trigger Recognition:
The petition became visible, but it is uncertain whether it was recognized as a trigger requiring interruption of the default path.

C_a — Cost Assumption:
The institutional redesign cost of reopening the conscription default does not appear to have been clearly assumed.

R_o — Reopening Act:
No operational alternative path appears to have been restored or opened.

P_r — Persistence of Re-entry:
No durable re-entry channel appears to have been established through which the issue could repeatedly modify the dominant path.
```

## Minimal conclusion

This case is best treated as a possible example of symbolic rupture rather than confirmed operational reopening.
