# Minimal Definitions

## P — Path

A selected operational route, rule, habit, stack, institution, or authority structure.

## E(P) — Exception Frequency

The frequency of cases requiring special handling, reinterpretation, or local repair within path P.

```text
E(P) = exception frequency of path P
```

## C_op(P) — Operational Cost

The expenditure required to continue along a presently selected path under current constraints.

```text
C_op(P) = cost to keep going
```

## C_rest(P) — Restoration Cost

The expenditure required to abandon, reopen, or revise a fixed path in favor of a prior or alternative configuration.

```text
C_rest(P) = cost to stop, step back, reopen, or choose otherwise
```

## Exception Compression

Reduction of cases requiring special handling, reinterpretation, or local repair.

## Low-Cost Convergence

A condition in which a path is repeatedly selected because operational continuation is cheaper than competing alternatives.

## F(P) — Path Fixation

The condition in which repeated selection makes a path structurally privileged and progressively harder to revise.

## R(P) — Repetition

Repeated selection or continuation of path P.
